6 Signal Assessment
●
Market Reality Strong
Supply chain intelligence category growing 34% YoY; post-2021 disruption demand pull is structural, not cyclical. Independent TAM of $4.2B confirmed.
●
Revenue Quality Strong
Customer independently confirmed expanding spend (+40% in 14 months). Gross margin 74%, improving. Top 3 customers = 28% of ARR — concentration manageable.
●
Defensibility Strong
Proprietary supplier dataset (3 years, 180K entities) compounds with each customer. Customer scored essentiality 9/10 and confirmed switching would require rebuilding 18 months of history.
●
Founder Clarity Strong
CEO knew CAC ($8,400), payback (11 months), and top 3 loss reasons unprompted. Proactively disclosed Q3 sales hire failure and the fix. No defensive answers under pressure.
◐
No Deal-Killers Flagged
No CFO — CEO managing finances. Cap table clean. One customer (Hartwell Group) at 19% of ARR with renewal in 6 months — needs independent verification before term sheet.
●
Exit Logic Coherent
Three named acquirers (SAP, Coupa, E2open) with specific integration rationale. Comparable: E2open acquired Logility at 7.2x ARR in 2022. Math works at current entry.

If You Advance — 3 Questions Still to Answer
01
Reconstruct monthly revenue for last 24 months from billing system exports and independently verify Hartwell Group contract renewal terms — their 19% ARR concentration and 6-month renewal window is the only structural risk not yet resolved.
02
Conduct a CFO-level financial review or require a fractional CFO hire as a close condition — CEO is managing a 74% gross margin business and $2.8M ARR without financial leadership, which creates reporting risk at Series A scale.
03
Validate the supplier dataset's proprietary depth with a technical audit — the moat claim rests entirely on 180K entity coverage being non-replicable. Confirm data sourcing agreements, exclusivity, and refresh methodology before pricing the defensibility premium into valuation.
