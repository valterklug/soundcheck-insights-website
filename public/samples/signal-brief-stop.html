The Opportunity
Nexpath reduces cart abandonment for mid-market DTC brands by replacing native checkout with a one-click flow that integrates across Shopify, WooCommerce, and custom stacks — promising 15–20% lift in conversion.
○ Evaluation ended — automatic stop condition triggered
6 Signal Assessment
◐
Market Reality Unclear
Checkout optimization is a real category but dominated by Bolt, Fast (failed), and native Shop Pay. Timing thesis — "Shopify dependency anxiety" — is directional but not independently validated.
○
Revenue Quality Weak
Stated NRR 112% contradicted by customer interview. One customer (Dunmore Co.) represents 44% of ARR, is actively evaluating Bolt, and has not renewed a contract expiring in 60 days.
○
Defensibility Weak
No proprietary data asset. Integration switching cost is 4–6 weeks of dev work per customer. Customer scored essentiality 5/10 and named two direct replacements they are actively testing.
◐
Founder Clarity Unclear
CEO knows product well but significantly overestimates NRR and retention. Lost deal explanations attributed entirely to price — no acknowledgment of competitive positioning gaps.
○
No Deal-Killers Blocked
Automatic stop: single customer at 44% of ARR with contract expiring in 60 days, confirmed by customer to be actively evaluating competitors. Not disclosed in pitch materials.
◐
Exit Logic Vague
Named acquirers are Shopify and Stripe — credible category players but no specific product integration rationale provided. Return math does not work if Dunmore churns.
What We Heard
We have an NRR of 112% — our customers consistently expand because the ROI on the conversion lift compounds over time. Retention hasn't been an issue for us.
CEO — Interview 1, revenue quality block
Honestly we're in conversations with Bolt right now. The lift we saw in year one didn't hold and support has gotten slower. Our contract is up in two months and we haven't decided yet.
Head of E-Commerce, Dunmore Co. — Interview 2
CEO claimed 112% NRR and described Dunmore as an "anchor customer and our strongest advocate." Customer confirmed active competitive evaluation and declining performance. Direct contradiction on Signal 2.
Contradiction Log — Interviews 1 & 2
Why We Are Ending the Evaluation
Automatic Stop Condition Triggered
Dunmore Co., independently confirmed as representing approximately 44% of Nexpath's ARR, has an active contract expiring in 60 days and is in a live competitive evaluation with Bolt Commerce. This was not disclosed in the pitch deck, data room, or Interview 1. The CEO described this customer as an "anchor advocate" with NRR of 112% — the customer interview directly contradicts both claims. The potential loss of a single customer representing nearly half of total ARR, combined with undisclosed concentration risk and an active NRR misrepresentation, meets the automatic stop threshold regardless of other signal quality.
